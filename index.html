WHO I AM

I'm a Computer Science graduate from Independent University Bangladesh (2018–2023), currently a Research Intern at the Center for Computation and Data Sciences (CCDS), IUB. My work sits at the collision point of quantum algorithms and practical machine learning.

On the quantum side, I design and benchmark Variational Quantum Classifiers and Quantum Kernel Methods using Qiskit and PennyLane, exploring hybrid quantum-classical frameworks for high-dimensional classification problems. On the engineering side, I build production-grade async microservices with Python ASGI frameworks, PostgreSQL, and CI/CD pipelines.

I believe quantum computing and AI together will reshape the infrastructure of civilization — and I intend to help build that future.

Gradient Masters at BLP-2025 Task 1: Advancing Low-Resource NLP for Bengali using Ensemble-Based Adversarial Training for Hate Speech Detection
Developed an ensemble-based adversarial training approach for Bengali hate speech detection in the BLP-2025 AACL-IJCNLP shared task. Leveraged BanglaBERT ensemble architectures with adversarial robustness techniques for low-resource NLP. The team placed 3rd and 6th across two submission tracks in the shared task.
EMNLP BLP '23
BaTEClaCor: A Novel Dataset for Bangla Text Error Classification and Correction
Introduced a curated dataset of 10,000 authentic Bangla comments from social media for error classification and correction. Benchmarked BanglaBERT achieving 79.1% binary and 74.1% multiclass accuracy. BanglaT5 achieved the best text correction performance with a Rouge-L score of 0.8459. The work contributes to preserving Bangla language authenticity in digital discourse and advances NLP for low-resource languages.
